About the artist

Hilary fell in love with photography at the age of 12, when she absconded with her mom’s Minolta 110 camera and never gave it back. Her passion has driven her photographic progress for over twenty years. Hilary’s authentic vision is the heart of her practice. From abstract photography to landscapes to weddings, she brings a unique, spontaneous vision to each photo.


Hilary currently lives and works in Alberta. Her formal training includes early years of working as a darkroom technician, freelance journalist photography, and gallery shows.

“It feeds my soul and my spirit, and I need to do that. You know sometimes you just choose to put the camera away and just look. But for me, I really enjoy the active part of it. I enjoy looking back.”

Hilary developed her artistic photography to new levels with a Canon film camera that could produce double exposures. Within a year, she had her first art show. She blended unrelated subjects, like fire and ice, or cityscapes and nature, into compelling new images.


double exposures

“When I was doing film I loved the serendipity of it. Not knowing what you got until you got it."

After a few years of exploring double exposures, landscape photography, and portraiture, Hilary became known in the community. Wedding photography was a natural next step. She’s been photographing weddings for close to 20 years. Hilary’s forte is capturing the joy, the spirit, and the special bond between the couple in a natural setting. Her candid style of documenting weddings reveals the life and celebration. Her earlier photography in film adds a special honesty and spontaneity to the photos.


wedding film photograpy

“To catch those those real moments when there's just raw unbridled - I don’t want to say passion, but you can just feel the connection between the couple.”

Living on the Black Cat guest ranch provides a wealth of inspiration. The natural beauty of the Rocky Mountains is a constant source of new discoveries, adventures and photographs. Not one to turn down the opportunity for an amazing shot, Hilary takes her equipment on multi-day horseback trips deep into the Willmore Wilderness.


nature photography

“I've got pretty good at cantering my horse and taking photos. Some people are like, “you could break that, you paid $1800 for it!” But I’m like, was it made to be used or to sit on the shelf at home?”

Blending wedding photography with the dramatic backdrops of the mountains was a natural continuation. Her most recent work shooting her daughter's wedding, riding across Jasper Lake on horseback, demonstrates this combination beautifully. The beauty of the landscape and the dynamic between the couple creates evocative images that capture the spirit of Hilary’s photography.


a natural union

“I guess uninhibited is what i’m looking for. Well what if my makeup is not perfect. What if you know? Who cares?”
